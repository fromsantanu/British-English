# Breads of England

Here’s a list of 20 words in the context of "Breads of England," along with their meanings and usage examples:

1. **Sourdough**  
   *Meaning*: A type of bread made from naturally fermented dough, giving it a tangy flavor.  
   *Usage*: "The sourdough bread from the local bakery had a perfect crust and a slightly tangy taste."

2. **Crumpet**  
   *Meaning*: A soft, spongy bread with a distinctive hole-filled texture, usually toasted and served with butter.  
   *Usage*: "We had warm crumpets for breakfast, topped with butter and jam."

3. **English Muffin**  
   *Meaning*: A small, round, flat yeast-leavened bread often split and toasted, commonly used for breakfast.  
   *Usage*: "English muffins are perfect for making eggs Benedict or simply spreading with butter."

4. **Hot Cross Bun**  
   *Meaning*: A sweet, spiced bun marked with a cross on top, traditionally eaten during Easter.  
   *Usage*: "The hot cross buns were filled with raisins and had a lovely, sticky glaze on top."

5. **Barm Cake**

   *Usage*: "I had a bacon barm for breakfast, a simple sandwich using the soft barm cake roll."

6. **Coburg**

   *Usage*: "The Coburg loaf had a thick, crunchy crust, perfect for soups and stews."

8. **Plaited Bread**
